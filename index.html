About Me

I’m a mechanical engineering student at UConn who’s been obsessed with technology and how things work for as long as I can remember. That curiosity is what pushed me toward engineering. Most recently, my obsession has landed me within the automotive industry, and it’s something I get to apply every day through UConn’s Formula SAE team, where I help bring our student-built race car to life. Being part of the team has taught me a ton about design, problem-solving, and what it feels like to work on something that really matters to a group of people.

When I’m not studying or in the shop, I’m often working on my own car. I love getting my hands dirty, learning by doing, and seeing the direct impact of every change I make. It’s helped me understand the practical side of engineering in a way that classroom work can’t always capture.

I also run my own media business, producing photo and video content for clients — including the Formula SAE team. It’s a different kind of creativity, but it’s taught me how to communicate ideas clearly, manage projects, and bring a story to life, whether that’s through a design, a part, or a camera lens.

Overall, I’m someone who likes building things, learning fast, and finding better ways to solve problems. I’m especially excited about opportunities in automotive engineering, motorsports, and any environment where I can combine hands-on work with thoughtful design.
