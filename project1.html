IC Powertrain - UConn Formula SAE

Project Background

This is more of a series of projects that are ongoing, rather than one. I'm a member of the IC Powertrain subsystem, so I work on our internal combustion engine and it's associated systems, producing the power for our IC car.

Objective(s)

The main objective of IC Powertrain is to bring out the IC car's peak performance capabilities, followed by finding ways to maintain proper operation and stability at peak performance.

Process

Recently, I was involved in tuning our car on the chassis dyno, where we analyzed engine behavior under load and fine-tuned parameters to optimize power delivery. I’ve also been contributing to ongoing testing on our engine dyno, running additional engines to compare performance, evaluate system changes, and gather data for future iterations.
Beyond tuning and testing, our team has been exploring the use of magnesium components — including a magnesium oil pan and differential mounts — as potential lightweight alternatives to improve overall vehicle efficiency.
A project that I was heavily involved in was the analysis of our current dry sump oil system. Our current oil tank is larger and heavier than we would like, and potentially being able to revamp our dry sump system can net substantial weight differences. The issue with this has been a lack of availability for oil tanks any smaller than we currently run, therefore leaving us to find something that is most likely too expensive, or designing one ourselves. Another issue we have run into is determining whether or not we not only shrink the size of our tank, but also decrease the amount of oil in the system. Unfortunately, testing oil capacities cannot be simiulated, and it would be too detrimental to the team as a whole if any of our engines were damaged during physical testing. This project does not yet have a conclusion, but it is something we tend to come back to every once in a while to discuss.

Conclusion

Working on these research and development efforts has given me insight into material selection, manufacturing considerations, and the tradeoffs that come with almost every decision. The experience I have with this team so far has been invaluable to my future possibilities.
